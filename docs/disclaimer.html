Plant Identification

FloraKnight uses the PlantNet API to identify plant species from photos. While PlantNet is a well-regarded scientific tool, no automated plant identification system is 100% accurate. Lighting conditions, photo angle, growth stage, and regional variation can all affect results.

Never consume, handle, or use a plant medicinally based solely on FloraKnight's identification. Always verify with a qualified botanist, local expert, or trusted printed field guide before acting on any result.

Safety Classifications

The edible, medicinal, toxic, and caution classifications in FloraKnight are provided for general informational purposes only.

  • A plant classified as "edible" may still cause reactions in some individuals.
  • A plant classified as "medicinal" is not a prescription or medical advice.
  • Toxic classifications may not cover all harmful parts or preparation methods.
  • An "unknown" classification means the species is not in our database — not that it is safe.

Disease Detection

The plant disease detection feature uses a machine learning model trained on the PlantVillage dataset, which covers 14 crop species. Results for plants outside this dataset may be inaccurate or misleading.

Disease detection results should not replace professional agronomic advice. For crop health concerns, consult an agricultural extension officer or qualified plant pathologist.

No Medical or Agricultural Advice

Nothing in FloraKnight constitutes medical advice, nutritional guidance, or professional agricultural advice. The app is an educational tool designed to assist plant discovery and awareness — not to replace expert judgment.
